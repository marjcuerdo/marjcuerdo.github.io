  • RaceTrack (in progress)

    Mid-Fi Report


    My Prototyping and Implementation group project is RaceTrack, a mobile and smartwatch application that helps runners stay motivated by getting detailed feedback on their performance and connecting them to their family, friends,and/or coaches for support during racetime through cheers. A user could create an account as a Runner, Supporter, or Coach, but would have the option to switch between modes.

    Each member of my group conducted an in-person think-aloud usability test with a participant on a medium-fidelity prototype of RaceTrack. The sessions were recorded through screen and audio capture, and data collection focused on interactions, navigational choices, and task completion success.

    I'm currently part of a 4-person group, where the workload for prototyping and data collection and analysis was split equally, but I'm primarily focused on implementing the Supporter role interfaces (displayed on the image).

  • PlayerSync

    Prototype


    Walkthrough Video


    Design Process

    PlayerSync was a solo term project for my Social Interaction Design course. I created a prototype in Adobe XD for a mobile app that matches the user to other gamers/players based on personal gaming preferences and behaviors. It aims to centralize communication across all existing gaming platforms.

    For this project, I created a persona, both low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes, and conducted a think-aloud observation and interview.
